09.06.2026 -- 16:00-17:00 (CEST, UTC+2)
Hasan Saad (LSU)
Atkin and Swinnerton-Dyer Congruences for Meromorphic Modular Forms

For holomorphic modular forms on congruence subgroups of $SL_2(\mathbb{Z})$, Hecke theory provides us with rich recurrence relations for Fourier coefficients of newforms. In the 1970s, Atkin and Swinnerton-Dyer conjectured that for noncongruence subgroups, similar recurrence relations hold p-adically. This was proved by Scholl and later extended by Scholl and Kazalicki to weakly holomorphic modular forms, where the coefficients of these recurrence relations are related to certain l-adic representations of Deligne. In this talk, we explain how this can be further extended to genuine meromorphic modular forms, and how these recurrence relations contain information about elliptic curves at the fibers described by the poles. Moreover, when the weight is small and the fiber of the universal elliptic curve at the pole is CM, we show how these long recurrence relations can be reduced to two-term recurrence relations involving the p-adic unit root.
